# Beacon Local — Voice & Tone Guide

## Who We Sound Like
A knowledgeable friend who happens to be really good at this. Warm, direct, honest. Not salesy, not corporate, not clever for the sake of clever. We talk like a real person explaining something they care about.

## Core Values (in order)
1. **Help patients find care** — this is the mission, not a tagline
2. **Help small practices compete** — the independent PT shouldn't need a marketing department
3. **Honesty and fairness** — in pricing, in SEO, in everything
4. **More than fair value** — the client should always be getting more than they're paying for

## Voice Rules

### Use "we" on the site
- "We" is the default voice — it's professional, scalable, and doesn't lock us into a headcount
- "I" is fine on the About page where the founder story lives
- Never promise a specific headcount ("one person does everything," "you deal with me directly")
- Promise the *experience*: "direct access," "no layers," "no ticket queues," "under one roof"

### Lead with the patient, not the competition
- The story is: patients are searching for help → they need to find the right provider → we make that happen
- Don't lead with "beat your competition" or "outrank your rivals"
- Competition can be mentioned as context, never as motivation
- Good: "Your patients are looking for you"
- Bad: "Your competitors are already online"

### Be honest about what we know
- We know local SEO, site architecture, GBP optimization, and content strategy for healthcare practices
- We do *not* claim to know pelvic floor PT as a clinical discipline
- Say "we understand the services" not "we know what services you offer"
- Say "specialize in" not "only work with" — expertise framing, not restriction
- Never list clinical terms as if they came from domain knowledge — they came from keyword research

### No em dashes in copy
- Don't use dashes (— or –) to insert asides in the middle of sentences
- If the aside is important, make it its own sentence. If it's not, cut it
- Dashes break the reader's flow and feel like marketing copy

### No exaggeration
- Don't oversell results ("dominate," "crush," "skyrocket")
- Don't make unprovable claims ("agency-level service," "best in class")
- Be honest about timelines: "most practices see results around month three"
- Be honest about limitations: "rankings drift without ongoing optimization"
- If we don't have testimonials, don't fabricate social proof

### No tricks, no gimmicks
- We do honest SEO — say it plainly
- Give real examples of what we won't do: "We won't stuff keywords into your Google Business Profile name"
- This is a selling point, not a disclaimer

## Pricing Language
- Prices are displayed openly — this is a competitive advantage
- Never imply prices are negotiable ("we'd rather charge less," "affordable pricing starting at")
- Frame the value: "fair pricing" and "you know exactly what you're paying for"
- Month-to-month, no contracts — frame as confidence in the work, not a sales tactic
- The package (website + SEO) is sold together — explain why, don't apologize for it

## Words and Phrases

### Use
- "get found" / "show up" (for search visibility)
- "your patients" / "the people searching for help"
- "under one roof" / "nothing falls through the cracks"
- "direct access" / "no layers"
- "honest SEO"
- "specialize in"
- "fair value"

### Avoid
- "marketing" (implies ads and services we don't offer)
- "dominate" / "crush" / "destroy" (aggressive, exaggerated)
- "nationwide" (don't claim geographic reach unless intentional)
- "only work with" (sounds restrictive, not specialized)
- "I know your industry" (we know local SEO, not clinical practice)
- "agency-level" (unprovable)
- "starting at" on the homepage (undersells the setup cost)
- "just" or "only" before the price (undermines the value)
- Anti-agency language (focus on what we do, not what others don't)

## Audience
- Pelvic floor physical therapists — solo practitioners and small clinics
- Intelligent, caring professionals who became PTs to help people
- Likely overwhelmed by the business side, especially online presence
- May have been burned by or skeptical of marketing agencies
- Inclusive: not just women's health — male pelvic floor PT, chronic pain, prenatal, postpartum

## The Emotional Hook
They became PTs to help people, not to learn SEO. We take that off their plate so they can focus on their patients. Every piece of copy should connect back to: patients searching → finding the right provider → getting help.

## Content Guidelines
- Every client site gets unique style, tone, and content — never reuse content across clients
- All AI-generated content is human-reviewed before publishing
- Client-facing content (blog posts, GBP updates) gets client approval at whatever level they choose
- Be specific about deliverables — vague promises erode trust
